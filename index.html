Hello! I'm Michelle Lee, a PhD candidate in Decision, Risk, and Financial Sciences at the Centre for Brain, Mind and Markets (University of Melbourne). My research uses behavioural and eye-tracking experiments to explore how complexity impacts financial decision-making and how we can design tools to help navigate these challenges.

Research interests: consumer finance, decision sciences, behavioural economics, experimental economics, complexity, and cognitive psychology.

Michelle holds a Bachelor of Commerce in Finance and Marketing and an Honours degree in Finance from the University of Melbourne. She has experience in portfolio risk management, academic research, and university teaching across finance, business analytics, and fintech.
